Matcha History

What is Matcha?

Matcha originated in China and was brought to Japan by a Buddhist monk in the 12th century. The process of making matcha involves steaming, drying, sorting, destemming, and grinding green tea leaves into a powder.

In other words... Matcha = Ground Up Green Tea

How to Make It

Instructions

Sift. Sift the matcha (to remove any lumps) into a mug or small bowl. Sifting matcha powder.

Whisk. Pour the 1⁄4 cup of hot water into the mug and use a matcha whisk to whisk vigorously in a ``W'' pattern for about 30 seconds, until frothy.

Fill. Fill up the mug. Add the remaining hot water and enjoy.

Tip: It is important to know the matcha to water ratio, which is... 2 grams of matcha powder to 70–80 grams of water.
